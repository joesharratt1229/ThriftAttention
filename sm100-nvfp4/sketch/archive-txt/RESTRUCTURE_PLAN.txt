12-warp softmax restructure: ATTEMPTED AND REVERTED (negative result)
======================================================================
Outcome: the shipped kernel remains the 8-softmax + 4-correction design
with the row-sum MMA offload and x64 S-load. Best measured (B200, H=16):
  S=8192:  0.464 ms (1.08x vs FA4 0.503)   S=16384: 1.563 ms (1.25x vs 1.953)
The 12-warp merge was implemented three ways, all bit-exact-validated,
and all slower. Details below so this isn't re-attempted blindly.

Hard constraint discovered (the crux):
  tcgen05.ld/st only allow a warp to access tmem lanes of its own
  sub-partition: warp k touches lanes [32*(k%4), 32*(k%4)+31] ONLY.
  A "helper" warp therefore CANNOT read S or write P rows belonging to
  another warp's sub-partition. Attempt 1 (helpers doing tmem ld/st for
  two row-groups) produced exactly-localized garbage in every foreign
  sub-partition access (rows 64-191 globally) -- correct rows matched
  bit-for-bit. This is why the 4-owners-per-stage shape is forced.

Attempt log (all at S=8192, baseline 0.463-0.464 ms):
  1. Helpers with direct tmem access, 2 row-groups each: ILLEGAL per
     sub-partition rule -> nondeterministic wrong results.
  2. Helpers as pure-ALU workers, owners keep all tmem I/O, block-3
     scores relayed owner->helper via smem, packed fp4 words relayed
     back; rescale moved into owner path; per-stage 192-thread named
     barriers 2x/iter:
       a. row-major smem relay [row][16]: 0.813 ms. 64B lane stride =
          16-way bank conflicts on every relay access.
       b. transposed [16][row] (conflict-free): 0.671 ms. Remaining loss
          is the rescale serialized into the owner critical path (it
          overlapped in separate warps in the baseline).
  3. Helpers = old correction role (rescale + epilogue, legal because
     warps 8-11 sit on sub-partitions 0-3) PLUS block-3 conversion via
     the smem relay; one global 12-warp barrier pair per iter; PO back
     to 256 (128 P-ready + 128 rescale-done): 0.587 ms. Still net loss:
     the ~25% conversion offload (64->48 cols) is outweighed by relay
     traffic + 2x 384-thread barriers per iteration, and rescale (fires
     ~60% of iters at 8k) rides the barrier chain instead of floating
     free on the PO count.

Persistent CTAs: ALSO ATTEMPTED AND REVERTED (negative result)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Implementation: grid = min(num_tiles, SM count); each CTA strides over
(batch, head, q_block) tiles; all mbarriers re-initialised per tile
between __syncthreads() (safe: the epilogue chain guarantees every
commit/TMA fired by end of tile); tmem allocated once, freed after the
loop. Bit-exact vs baseline, deterministic, no hangs -- but SLOWER:
8k 0.464 -> 0.506 ms, 16k 1.750 -> 1.891 ms (interleaved, sustained).

Why it cannot win at this tile size: with equal-cost 256-row tiles the
static stripe gives some CTAs ceil(T/148) tiles and some one fewer --
exactly the same wave-quantization loss the hardware scheduler already
has (loss = (ceil(T/S)*S - T)/(ceil(T/S)*S), e.g. 13.5% at T=512).
Dynamic work-stealing via an atomic converges to the same distribution
for uniform tiles. Meanwhile the software tile switch (2x syncthreads +
47 mbarrier re-inits + full pipeline drain/refill) is more expensive
than the hardware CTA switch it replaces. Net: pure overhead.
The tail loss is only reducible by (a) smaller tiles -- the 128-row
single-Q-stage retile quarters the quantization error at 16k+, or
(b) split-KV partial-attention with a combine pass (flash-decoding
style) for small batch*heads*q_blocks.

LANDED (from the FA4 source analysis): lazy rescale + packed f32x2
------------------------------------------------------------------
1. Lazy rescale (FA4 rescale_threshold, softmax.py:300-319 equivalent):
   row_max only advances when a tile max exceeds it by LAZY_RESCALE_T=4
   log2 units; acc_scale is exactly 1.0 otherwise so correction rarely
   fires.  e4m3 headroom reserved via P_SCALE_RANGE = 448/16; the factor
   cancels exactly in the row-sum normalisation (ones-MMA shares the sf).
   The old fminf(block_max, row_max) was removed: P is block-relative
   (it always was; the fmin was a no-op pre-lazy but WRONG post-lazy).
2. Packed f32x2: ta_fma2/ta_mul2 (fma.rn.f32x2 / mul.f32x2) in the
   score->P chain, O rescale, and epilogue normalise.  NOTE: max.f32x2
   does not exist on sm100 (ptxas rejects); floors stay scalar FMNMX.
   SASS shows ptxas already auto-packs many pairs (FMUL2 x808 vs our 64
   FFMA2), which is why the manual win is modest.
Results (interleaved, sustained, H=16): 4k 0.130->0.119 (1.12x vs FA4),
8k 0.464->0.442 (1.15x), 16k 1.754->1.704 (1.06x), 32k ~6.7 (1.29x).
Accuracy unchanged (cos 0.9564/0.9558), deterministic, no hangs.
Rejected while here: dropping the fmaxf floor (~3% more) -- reopens the
magic-number aliasing hazard (underflow aliases to satfinite 6.0) on
adversarial inputs.

Conclusions / where the remaining headroom actually is:
  - Post-rowsum ablation shape: conversion ALU ~35-40%, S tmem loads
    ~20%, rescale ~10%, MMA+TMA+barrier shell ~33%. The conversion can
    only be spread to more warps through smem, and the relay+barrier tax
    eats the gain. Do not retry variants of this without changing the
    data flow itself.
  - Rescale frequency is the cheapest remaining target: a thresholded /
    deferred correction (tolerate the row max lagging by <= 1 log2 step;
    top-bucket P compresses 8->6 in e2m1) would cut ~60% of rescale
    firings at the cost of a small, evaluable accuracy hit.
  - Bigger steps require retiling: KV_TILE=128 with a single Q stage
    (tmem cannot hold 2 stages of 128-col S), which halves per-tile
    barrier/SF overhead and rescale frequency per element; or software-
    pipelining the S load against the previous iteration's conversion.
  - Register budget knowledge: softmax path is spill-free at 152 regs
    (144 costs ~1.5%); the EXP_APPROX bit-trick softmax needs no
    MUFU.EX2 or ex2-emu tables. Freed units are only useful if some
    future design needs a fatter warp group elsewhere.

RETILE LANDED: KV_TILE=128, single Q stage, split-P (2026-07-03)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Shipped design: 128-row Q tile, KV=128 as two K=64 chunks, two full-width
S buffers ping-ponged across iterations (tmem: O 128 + S 2x128 + OSUM/SFs
= ~436/512), P + sf_p written into consumed S columns (chunk c at
S + c*64 + 0..11).  Softmax pairs: warp w owns chunk 0 / rows 32w..+31
(stats, lazy rescale, epilogue), warp w+4 converts chunk 1 for the same
rows (legal: same tmem sub-partition), coordinated by two 64-thread named
barriers per tile through smem (partner max in, m_used out).  PV chunk 0
launches off P0Ready (count 128) while chunk 1 still converts; P1Ready
(count 256) also carries the owners' rescale-done arrivals.  wg2 idles.
V TMA is 2x64-wide boxes (32B-swizzle constraint); K sf atoms are per-128
rows (extension k_sf_atoms is now [b,h,kv/128,2,512]); V sf atoms stay
per-64 and are consumed two at a time.

Make-or-break lessons:
1. QK-prefetch hoist: issuing QK(iter+1) BEFORE the P0(iter) wait is the
   difference between a regression and the win (0.490 -> 0.366 ms @8k).
   With one softmax stream, SFull(iter+1) must fire while tile iter is
   still converting or the whole S-load latency is exposed per tile.
2. Register regions: parking idle wg2 at setmaxnreg_dec<24> makes ptxas
   fail allocation kernel-wide (C7600 at any softmax target); keep every
   warpgroup at >= 80.  Softmax runs at 176 (8*176 + 8*80 = 2048 exactly).
3. Consolidating both sf words + both barriers onto the owner warp is
   SLOWER (0.392 vs 0.360): the owner is the critical path; keep the
   partner doing its own sf word behind the second barrier.

Results (interleaved, sustained, H=16, vs FA4):
  4k:  0.112 ms  1.19x     8k:  0.360 ms  1528 TF/s  1.43x
  16k: 1.532 ms  1.21x     32k: 6.617 ms  ~1.25-1.5x (32k FA4 throttles)
Previous best (KV=64): 0.119 / 0.442 / 1.704 / 6.7 ms.
cos vs SDPA 0.956 unchanged, deterministic, no hangs (default x5, minimum
128x128 shape, GQA 8:2).  KV64 snapshot: scratchpad fp4_attention_kv64_best.cu.

Remaining (unchanged): drain-free persistence, 2-CTA cluster, causal/
split-KV, e2e quantise fusion.

DRAIN-FREE PERSISTENCE LANDED (2026-07-03)
------------------------------------------
FA4-style persistent CTAs done right this time: grid = min(tiles, SMs),
NO barrier re-init, NO tile-boundary syncthreads.  A CTA-local cumulative
iteration counter g keys the whole KV/S pipeline (slot = 2*(g%8), phases
(g/8)&1, buf = g&1, (g>>1)&1) so phases simply continue across tiles --
this also handles odd kv_iters (buffer mapping shifts per tile) for free.
Per-tile resources got two new mbarriers: OEmpty (owners release O/O_sum
after the epilogue reads; mma waits before each tile's first PV, parity
(lt&1)^1) and OStore (store warp releases the smem O staging buffer after
its TMA read completes; owners wait before writing).  Q smem reuse rides
QFull/QEmpty per tile (parity lt&1).  Only the load warp and the store
warp re-derive tile addresses (set_tile); mma and softmax are address-free.
Rescale is skipped at iter==0 of each tile (O is overwritten by the first
PV; an iter-0 rescale would also touch the previous tile's O unprotected).

Validated: default x5, odd kv_iters (kv=384), multi-tile+odd (q=2048 H=16
kv=1152, 256 tiles > 148 SMs), min 128x128, GQA 8:2 -- no hangs,
deterministic, cos unchanged.

Results (interleaved, sustained, H=16, vs FA4):
  4k:  0.112 -> 0.106 ms  1.25x      8k:  0.360 -> 0.355 ms  1549 TF/s  1.47x
  16k: 1.532 -> 1.541 ms  1.19x (flat, tail already tiny at 13.8 waves)
  32k: 6.617 -> 6.318 ms  ~1.5x
Contrast with the FAILED KV64 persistence attempt (re-init + drain lost
4-9%): the win is entirely in not draining -- phase continuation plus the
OEmpty/OStore handoff overlaps tile t's epilogue with tile t+1's start.

Remaining: 2-CTA cluster (halve KV traffic, M=256), causal masking /
split-KV, e2e quantise+pack fusion, MUFU offload experiment (declined for
now).

2-CTA CLUSTER MMA: DESIGN SKELETON (in progress)
------------------------------------------------
Strategy: build as a SECOND kernel variant (compile-time CTA_GROUP
branch), host dispatch on num_tiles vs SM count -- 2-CTA coarsens
scheduling to 74 cluster-slots and 256-row effective tiles, so it should
lose at <=4k and win at >=8-16k; keep the 1-CTA path for small shapes
and tune the crossover empirically (FA4-table style).
Per-CTA structure PRESERVED: each CTA of the pair keeps its own 128 Q
rows, softmax pairs, S buffers, O accumulator + row sums, epilogue.
What changes: QK/PV become one cta_group::2 M=256 instruction issued by
the leader CTA; K/V held once per pair (split/multicast per the
mechanics report); MMA-completion and P-ready barriers become
cluster-scoped.  Mechanics extraction from FA4 + CuTe DSL sources is
running; key unknowns: B-operand sourcing across the pair, SFA/SFB
placement for block-scaled 2-CTA MMA, commit multicast form, remote
mbarrier arrivals.

2-CTA CLUSTER MMA: DETAILED IMPLEMENTATION + DISPATCH PLAN
(written at session end 2026-07-03; next session starts here)
================================================================
Baseline to beat (interleaved, sustained, H=16, vs FA4):
  4k 0.112ms/1.19x | 8k 0.351ms/1.46x | 16k 1.522ms/1.21x | 32k ~6.6ms/~1.5x
Cost structure at 8k: shell 53% (MMA+TMA+mbarrier chains), conversion 37%
(partially hidden by split-P), S-loads 9%.  2-CTA attacks the shell: one
M=256 QK/PV per cluster instead of two M=128s, and K/V held once per pair.

A. DISPATCH ARCHITECTURE
------------------------
1. Two kernel variants from one source: template/ifdef on FA4_CTA_PAIR
   (0 = current 1-CTA persistent kernel, UNTOUCHED; 1 = cluster variant).
   Compile both into the extension; nvfp4_sm100_attention_launch picks.
2. Host heuristic: num_tiles = batch*heads*(q_len/128).
   - num_tiles < DISPATCH_MIN_TILES  -> 1-CTA variant (tail-sensitive).
   - else                            -> 2-CTA variant.
   Initial DISPATCH_MIN_TILES ~ 8*sm_count (i.e. >= ~8 waves of cluster
   slots); tune empirically at 4k/8k/16k after the variant works.  Expect
   2-CTA to LOSE at <=4k (74 cluster slots, 256-row effective tiles double
   the wave-quantization tail) and win at >=8-16k.
3. 2-CTA launch: cudaLaunchKernelEx with cudaLaunchAttributeClusterDimension
   = (2,1,1) (or __cluster_dims__(2,1,1) on the kernel); grid.x = even,
   = min(2*ceil(num_tiles/2)... , 2*floor(sm_count/2)); persistent tile
   loop assigns one 256-row SUPER-TILE (two adjacent 128-row q-blocks of
   the same head) per cluster: super_tiles = batch*heads*(q_len/256);
   CTA rank r takes rows 128*r of the super-tile.  q_len%256 fallback:
   dispatch odd-q shapes to the 1-CTA variant (keep it simple).

B. WHAT IS PRESERVED PER CTA (deliberately)
-------------------------------------------
Each CTA of the pair keeps, exactly as in the current kernel: its own 128
Q rows (Q TMA + SFQ), softmax owner/partner pairs + named barriers + lazy
rescale, both S buffers, its own O accumulator + OSUM + ones-MMA rowsum,
epilogue + OEpi/OEmpty/OStore chain, drain-free persistence counters (gi).
The D/A operands of a cta_group::2 MMA span the pair's tmem halves, so
softmax/epilogue code does not change: each CTA reads its own tmem lanes.

C. WHAT CHANGES
---------------
1. MMA issue: only the LEADER CTA (cluster rank 0) elected thread runs
   mma_loop; instructions become tcgen05.mma.cta_group::2 (idesc M field
   = 256>>7).  The follower's warp 12 parks (but see barriers below --
   it may need to relay arrivals).
2. K/V: held ONCE per pair.  Two candidate schemes (mechanics report was
   not finished -- verify against FA4 source, see F):
   (a) split: each CTA's load warp TMAs half of each K/V tile into its
       own smem; the 2-CTA MMA's B descriptor sources both halves via
       DSMEM.  This HALVES KV smem per CTA -> either double prefetch
       depth or reclaim ~72KB smem.
   (b) multicast: cp.async.bulk.tensor with cluster multicast mask loads
       the full tile into both CTAs (no smem saving, still halves DRAM/L2
       traffic).  Simpler barriers; try (b) first if (a)'s descriptor
       semantics are unclear.
3. Barriers that become cluster-scoped:
   - KVFull: leader must observe both CTAs' TMA completions (remote mbar
     arrival via shared::cluster addressing / TMA to leader's mbar).
   - SFull-equivalent: leader's commit must wake softmax in BOTH CTAs:
     tcgen05.commit.cta_group::2 has an mbarrier multicast form (FA4 uses
     it via PipelineUmmaAsync) -- find exact PTX in cutlass tcgen05/mma.py.
   - P0Ready/P1Ready: both CTAs' softmax must signal the leader: remote
     arrive on the leader's mbar (mapa.shared::cluster + mbarrier.arrive
     .shared::cluster), counts double (256/512).
   - OFull per CTA (each CTA's epilogue) from the leader's commit multicast.
   - fence.mbarrier_init.release.cluster already in place; add
     cluster.sync() (barrier.cluster.arrive/wait) at kernel start before
     any cross-CTA mbar use, and before tmem dealloc at the end.
4. tmem: verify whether alloc must be tcgen05.alloc.cta_group::2 (likely
   both CTAs execute it) and whether TMEM_COLS accounting changes.
5. Scale factors (CRITICAL, block-scaled mxf4nvf4): where do SFA and SFB
   live for cta_group::2?  Likely SFA per-CTA (each CTA's tmem holds sf
   for its 128 A rows -- our SFQ/SFP layout already per-CTA) and SFB
   duplicated in both CTAs' tmem (our SFK/SFV cps run in both).  VERIFY
   in cutlass tcgen05 mma.py (kind mxf4nvf4, cta_group=2) before coding.

CORRECTNESS FIXES LANDED 2026-07-04 (pre-2-CTA), perf unchanged
----------------------------------------------------------------
The kernel was NONDETERMINISTIC run-to-run (0.65% of outputs, |diff| up
to ~2 at 8k) from two latent races, both now fixed:
1. Missing tcgen05.fence::before_thread_sync (HANG_FIX known-issue #2):
   softmax P/sf tmem stores were published via P0Ready/P1Ready arrives,
   and epilogue O tmem reads via OEpi/OEmpty, with wait::st/ld only.
   Added tcgen05_fence_before() at all three arrive sites.
2. Rescale-vs-PV race: the QK-prefetch hoist issues QK(g) BEFORE PV(g-1),
   so SFull(g)'s commit no longer implies PV(g-1) completed (the old code
   comment predated the hoist).  A lazy rescale at iter g could then RMW
   O concurrently with PV(g-1)'s accumulation.  Fix: on the rare rescale
   branch, wait SEmpty(buf(g-1)) parity (pg>>1)&1 -- committed right after
   PV1(g-1) -- before touching O.  Deterministic across 6 runs after; perf
   0.353/1.358 ms at 8k/16k, within noise of the pre-fix kernel.

  its own softmax warps' slowed busy chain.  Per-lane remote arrives were
  the first ~15% of the loss (fixed: fence + __syncwarp + one elected
  count-32 cluster arrive per warp -- 0.56x -> 0.63x); the remaining ~1.6x
  softmax slowdown is UNEXPLAINED but consistent with tmem/DSMEM bandwidth
  contention: every pair MMA pulls half of B from the PEER CTA's smem across
  the cluster fabric, and the morning ablation showed tmem read bandwidth is
  THE critical resource (S-load removal alone was -46%).  Bisection of the
  slowdown (sload/convert ablations under --pair) was not finished.

Hard-won mechanics for any retry (all found the hard way, all on B200
cuda 13.0 driver 580.126.09):
  1. ptxas forbids MIXING cta_group::1 and ::2 tcgen05 ops in one kernel;
     if constexpr-discarded branches are fine (that is why the template
     builds).  tcgen05.ld/st/wait/fence are group-less and shared.
  2. cta_group::2 MMA requires N >= 16 (step 16).  The N=8 ones/rowsum MMA
     traps ILLEGAL INSTRUCTION -- and the trap surfaces at the mma warp's
     NEXT mbarrier wait (async pipe), which cost hours of misattribution.
     Fix: rowsum N=16, OSUM widened to 16 tmem cols (only col 0 is read).
  3. mbarrier.try_wait.parity.acquire.CLUSTER: ptxas accepts, sm_100a traps
     at runtime.  Use cta-scope waits; CUTLASS's own cluster pipelines do.
  4. A cta_group::2 tensor-TMA's mbar operand must EXPLICITLY address the
     pair leader in cluster space (mapa to even rank; FA4 does
     `and addr, ~0x1000000`).  A plain local offset does NOT auto-route:
     leader starves (T1 hang before the fix).
  5. Plain non-tensor cp.async.bulk rejects .cta_group::2, and its
     shared::cluster remote-mbar form does NOT deliver tx cross-CTA on this
     part (T3 HANG).  Scale-factor atoms must ride tensor TMAs: 256B-row
     tmap (TMA box caps at 256 elems/dim), box 256x4 = one tile's 2 atoms.
  6. tcgen05.cp.cta_group::2 issued by the leader reads EACH CTA's OWN smem
     at the descriptor offset and fills EACH CTA's OWN tmem; commit
     multicast (mask 3) fires the local barrier in both CTAs (T2 PASS).
     So the leader cps sf for the whole pair; no follower relay needed.
  7. Per-lane remote cluster arrives are prohibitively slow; aggregate to
     one count-32 arrive per warp (fence::before_thread_sync, __syncwarp,
     elected lane arrives with count).
  8. TMA tensor boxes cap each dim at 256 elements (a 512-wide sf box is
     cudaErrorInvalidValue at encode time).

Verdict: matches the same-day breakdown prediction -- the kernel is softmax-
chain-bound, so halving shell issue/barrier count cannot pay (predicted
ceiling ~15-20%; measured -40% because the pair fabric traffic additionally
slows the softmax chain).  Do NOT retry 2-CTA until (a) the softmax busy
chain (S-load bandwidth + convert) is structurally cheaper, and (b) the
tmem/DSMEM contention hypothesis is confirmed or killed by finishing the
--pair ablation bisection from the snapshot.  Note FA4's own bf16 fwd runs
2-CTA at these shapes profitably because its balance point differs (no fp4
convert chain in softmax; MUFU-based exp).

INT_ENCODE VARIANT: INTEGER-DOMAIN E2M1 ENCODE (implemented 2026-07-04,
UNVALIDATED -- written on the Mac, needs a B200 run via modal_bench.py)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Derivation (why the pow2+cvt chain is a fixed re-encoding):
  r = fma(score, scale_log2, TA_MAGIC - M) lands in [2^23, 2^24) where the
  fp32 ulp is 1.0, so RN snaps the block-relative log2 to an INTEGER v and
  bits(r) = 0x4B400000 + v.  pow2x4 then builds p = 2^(v+2) -- an EXACT
  power of two -- and cvt.rn.satfinite.e2m1x2 quantises it.  The only
  rounding in the whole tail is the v = -4 tie (p = 0.25, exactly halfway
  between 0 and 0.5), which RN-even resolves to 0.  Code table:
      v:    0   -1   -2   -3   -4   <=-5     (p = 4, 2, 1, 0.5, 0.25, ...)
      code: 6    4    2    1    0    0
  NOT affine (the 0.5 subnormal breaks the line) -> byte-LUT via PRMT.
Implementation (fp4_attention_sm100.cu):
  - ta_encode_8_e2m1_int(): per 8 scores.  (a) signed-int max of the float
    bits against 0x4B400000 -- replaces BOTH the fmaxf floor and the
    magic-aliasing guard (bit patterns are monotonic for positive floats;
    negatives/underflows compare below as signed ints).  NaN caveat: NaN
    bits compare ABOVE the floor; finite-fp4 QK cannot produce NaN.
    (b) +4 LUT-index bias folded into the addend: block_addend becomes
    (TA_MAGIC_X2 + 4.0f) - block_row_max, exact (integer floats < 2^24),
    so the low byte of the clamped bits IS u = v+4 in [0,4].  (c) PRMT
    gather to nibble-packed selectors, PRMT LUT (0x04020100 / 0x06060606;
    entries 5-7 = 6 reproduce satfinite, future-proof for deferred-
    correction v=+1), nibble pack.  Output word bit-identical to
    ta_cvt_8xf32_to_e2m1_packed(p1,p0,p3,p2,p5,p4,p7,p6) (element i in
    nibble i; cvt.e2m1x2 d,a,b packs a high / b low).
  - Kernel is now template<bool INT_ENCODE> (extern "C" dropped; symbol
    only referenced by the launcher); convert_p branches if constexpr.
    Base instantiation is byte-identical logic to the shipped kernel.
  - Dispatch: nvfp4_sm100_attention_launch grew an int_encode arg;
    extension exposes attention_only(..., int_encode=False) and
    quantise_and_attention(..., int_encode=False) (defaulted: existing
    callers incl. profile_breakdown.py unchanged).
Expected effect (pipe rebalance, not op-count reduction): per 8 elements
the old tail is 8 FMNMX (FMA pipe, shared with the FFMA2s + rescale/epi
mul2) + ~8-16 int + 4 CVT (cvt pipe) + pack movs; the new tail is ~29
ALU-pipe ops (8 IMNMX + 9 PRMT + 12 SHF/LOP3), zero FMA-pipe, zero CVT.
Win iff ALU/PRMT has headroom in the softmax warps and/or cvt.e2m1x2 is
slow-rate; loss if ALU saturates.  That question is exactly what the A/B
answers; also nets back the ~3% previously rejected for dropping the floor
(the clamp now rides the mandatory LUT-index clamp).
Validation gates (in order, on B200):
  1. bench_attention.py biteq column: torch.equal(base, int) on EVERY
     shape -- strict re-encoding, any diff is a bug (first suspects:
     nibble order, the v=-4 tie, PRMT selector nibble layout).
  2. Hang matrix (unchanged protocol) on the int variant: default x5,
     kv=384, q=2048 H=16 kv=1152, kv_iters=1 multi-tile, 128x128, GQA 8:2.
  3. Interleaved perf (bench_attention.py prints base | int | fa4).
  4. SASS audit: nvdisasm the convert region of the <true> instantiation;
     LUT constants + floor hoisted, IMNMX is signed, PRMTs not re-expanded.

CVT_X4 VARIANT: cvt.rs.e2m1x4 CONVERT TAIL (implemented 2026-07-08,
UNVALIDATED -- written on the Mac, needs a B200 run)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Motivation: the INT_ENCODE lesson says convert cost = ISSUE SLOTS, and the
post-stream lesson says what survives is "reduce convert INSTRUCTION
COUNT".  Full integer-domain packing lost because its LUT/merge tax (~29
ALU ops/8) exceeds the cvt hardware's built-in pack.  This variant keeps
the cvt hardware doing the packing but converts FOUR elements per
instruction instead of two, and pins the pow2 reconstruct to one IMAD:

  cvt.rs{.relu}.satfinite.e2m1x4.f32 d, {a, b, e, f}, rbits;   (PTX 8.7+,
  sm_100a/103a; CUDA >= 12.8 -- container is 13.0.1, OK)

Bit-identity argument (gate 1 must show biteq=True, any diff is a bug):
  - PTX: ".rs: ... result is rounded in the direction toward zero or away
    from zero based on the carry out of the integer addition of the
    supplied random bits (rbits) to the truncated off (discarded) bits of
    mantissa from the input."  rbits = 0 => carry never fires => round
    toward zero.
  - Every input to the pack is p = 2^(v+2), v in [-126, 0] (exact e2m1
    value or below range): discarded bits are all-zero => conversion exact
    under ANY rounding.  The only rounding case in the base path, v = -4
    (p = 0.25, discarded bits "10..."), gives no carry => 0, matching
    cvt.rn's tie-to-even => 0.  Values below (v <= -5, incl. the clamped
    floor 2^-124) truncate to 0 = rn result.
  - Nibble order (PTX): "the value converted from inputs a, b, e, f are
    stored in each 4 bits starting from upper bits of d", i.e. a ->
    d[15:12] ... f -> d[3:0].  Helper lists (f3, f2, f1, f0) so element i
    lands in nibble i; word = mov.b32 {lo, hi} keeps elements 0-7 ->
    nibbles 0-7 as in the base path.  (Base x2 packs a HIGH / b low, hence
    its swapped-pair call sites; the x4 helper takes plain element order.)
Instruction accounting per 8 elements (tail after the 4 FFMA2):
  base:  8 FMNMX + 8x(SHF+IADD | LEA) + 4 CVT(e2m1x2) + ~3 pack movs
         = 23-31 issue slots
  x4:    8 FMNMX + 8 IMAD (mad.lo pinned) + 2 CVT.RS(e2m1x4) + 1 mov.b32
         = 19 issue slots
  => -17% to -39% of the convert tail; convert+P-store ablation is ~40% of
  wall, so expect roughly -4% to -10% wall if CVT.RS is single-SASS and
  issue-bound reasoning holds.  FMNMX floor is retained (no min/max.f32x2
  exists -- LLVM lowers v2f32 maxnum to two scalar max.f32 even on sm_100;
  matches the FMNMX2-free SASS audit).  Register pressure neutral (same 8
  live p floats; constants fold into IMAD imm / RZ).
Implementation (mirrors INT_ENCODE plumbing):
  - ta_pow2x4_from_bits_mad(): mad.lo.u32 br*0x800000 + 0x40800000 (the
    C++ shift+add left ptxas free to emit SHF+IADD).
  - ta_score_to_p2_mad(): same fma2 + fmaxf floor, mad reconstruct.
  - ta_cvt_8xf32_to_e2m1_packed_x4(): 2x cvt.rs.satfinite.e2m1x4.f32
    (rbits = 0) + mov.b32; args in element order.
  - Kernel is template<bool CVT_X4> (extern "C" dropped, launcher-only
    symbol); convert_p branches if constexpr; <false> is byte-identical
    logic to the shipped kernel.
  - nvfp4_sm100_attention_launch grew int cvt_x4 (0/1) BEFORE stream --

PIPE2: ITERATION-INTERLEAVED SOFTMAX (2026-07-08): BIT-EXACT, 0.87x -> REVERTED
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Idea: remove the per-iteration owner/partner rendezvous entirely.  Warp w
handles the EVEN global iterations whole (both 64-col chunks, S buffer A
fixed since buf == g&1), w+4 the ODD ones (buffer B); the only remaining
cross-iteration dependency -- the lazy row-max chain m(g-1) -> m(g) -- rides
hm_used[row] behind a per-row-group MStats mbarrier pair (arrive.release
publishes the smem store; posted right after the stats step).  The two named
barriers per iteration disappear; P0Ready/P1Ready counts and the mma/load/
store warps are untouched (the iteration's handler arrives P1Ready twice).
In-warp order: ld c0 -> reduce c0 -> convert c0 (block-relative, no m
needed) -> ld c1 reusing the 64 score regs (128 live floats would spill) ->
reduce c1 -> baton -> stats/rescale -> sf words -> arrives -> convert c1.
Motivation: "software-pipeline S(g+1) load against convert(g)" is impossible
in-warp (tcgen05.wait::ld waits on ALL outstanding loads) but trivial across
warps -- each warp has its own ld scoreboard.  Epilogue/TmemDealloc stay on
warps 0-3; odd kv_iters flip each warp's starting parity per tile (g is
global under drain-free persistence); kv_iters==1 leaves one warp of each
pair idle per tile.  MStats can never alias a phase: its next arrival always
sits behind the previous reader's completed iteration (in-tile via the baton
itself, across tiles via OFull->epilogue on w / the mma tile preamble).

Result (B200, validate_stream.py): bit-exact on the full ladder incl. GQA
and kv_iters=1 multi-tile, det x5, no hangs -- but uniformly SLOWER:
  2k 0.870x | 4k 0.884x | 8k 0.869x (0.352 -> 0.405 ms) | 16k 0.862x
Profile (--fine, 8k): softmax wait-for-S grew 26% -> 35-36% while busy FELL
to ~62% -- the loss is pure exposed SFull latency, not issue collisions.
(Also from this profile: S tmem load segment is only ~3% of softmax span;
the 47% sload ablation share is dependency/issue structure, not port time.
mma warp: 47% waiting on softmax, 28% on KVFull slack, 24% issuing.  Load
warp 70% parked on KVEmpty backpressure = TMA queue far ahead; DRAM idle.)

THE MECHANISM (the valuable part): with 2 S buffers and P stored INSIDE the
consumed S buffer, QK(g+2) is gated on SEmpty(g) = "PV1(g) complete", i.e.
on the handler's OWN final arrive.  Interleaved, each warp therefore pays
the full softmax -> PV1 -> QK -> SFull round-trip PRIVATELY once per handled
iteration; the old pair structure only needs S one iteration ahead and the
prefetch hoist covers that.  Confirmed by construction, not just data:
tcgen05.commit(SFull) tracks ALL prior async ops of the mma thread (incl.
the PVs issued before it), so at prefetch distance 1 SFull can never beat
the PV round-trip NO MATTER what barrier gates QK.  => The shipped kernel's
own 26% SFull wait is this same loop.  Attack the loop, not the softmax
schedule.  Do not re-attempt softmax-side rescheduling (in-warp OR
cross-warp) while S depth is 2 and P lives inside S.

EARLYQK: P PLANES OUT OF S + SFree-GATED DISTANCE-2 QK (2026-07-08):
BIT-EXACT, 0.91x -> REVERTED
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Follow-up to PIPE2's mechanism finding, on the UNCHANGED softmax schedule:
P/sf_p moved to dedicated tmem planes (436..483, 12 cols per (buf, chunk)),
a new SFree[buf] mbarrier (count 256) armed by each softmax warp right
after its x64 S load, QK(g+2) issued at the TOP of mma iteration g gated on
SFree instead of SEmpty (2-QK tile preamble), and a compensating WAR wait
(SEmpty[buf], parity ph^1) in the softmax warps before convert_p overwrites
the P planes.  Bit-exact on the full ladder, det x5, no hangs.
  2k 0.923x | 4k 0.919x | 8k 0.911x (0.353 -> 0.387 ms) | 16k 0.907x
Why it lost ~9%: the softmax critical chain gained 4 ops per warp-iteration
(SFree fence+arrive, WAR try_wait+fence) and the pacing loop was NOT the QK
gate: the mma warp still parks once per iteration (now on SFree(g), the
softmax loads, instead of SEmpty) before it can issue anything, and the
added softmax ops price straight into the wall (chain-paced, ~1:1).

REVISED MODEL OF THE 26% SFull WAIT (both failures + the profile agree):
the pacer is the mma warp's serial park/wake chain -- ~5 mbarrier waits per
iteration (KVFull-K, SEmpty, KVFull-V, P0, P1), each park/wake costing
~O(100-300) cycles on the supply path -- NOT MMA exec (tensor pipe ~20%
busy), NOT DRAM (load warp 70% ahead), NOT the softmax issue count alone.
Lever: REMOVE WAITS from the mma warp's loop, don't re-gate them.

QK2: BOTTOM-OF-LOOP DISTANCE-2 QK PREFETCH (2026-07-08): BIT-EXACT, ~1.01x
-> NOT KEPT (reverted with the session's other changes; the ~1% is real if
ever wanted -- re-apply recipe at the end of this entry)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
The minimal extraction from the two failures: mma-loop-only.  The QK
prefetch moves from the top of iteration g (QK(g+1), warp PARKED on
SEmpty until PV1(g-1) completed) to the bottom (QK(g+2), right after
PV1(g)'s issue), where it needs NO recycle wait at all: P1Ready(g) --
just passed -- implies softmax(g) finished reading S[buf] (wait::ld
precedes the arrives), and the tensor pipe orders the S overwrite after
PV1(g)'s P read (same-thread issue order); commit(SFull(g+2)) tracking
PV1(g) is a full iteration of slack.  Tile preamble seeds BOTH S buffers
(2 QKs, no SEmpty wait: previous tile's PV1s are pipe-prior).  SEmpty
survives only for the softmax rescale branch.  Softmax code untouched.
